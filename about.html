Founder's Story

“Generosity consists not of the sum given, but the manner in
which it is bestowed.”

Growing up playing basketball in Africa. Adeola Ajayi was able to secure a basketball scholarship to the United States.
This is a dream come true for him and a lot of kids that were able to attain this same opportunity. However, upon his arrival in 2009, Adeola started to notice sharp pains on his both shins. It was later discovered that he had a stressed fracture on both legs, in which he had to undergo two major surgery in the same week.
Looking back into the time he spent back in Africa. Adeola realized the factors to his injury, was playing on concrete flooring throughout his childhood without good basketball shoes.
Adeola drew upon these personal experiences and founded the Adeola Ajayi Foundation – a non-profit organization with the goal of changing this reality and the lives of countless boys and girls on the African continent through the power of sport.
Our organization is to travel to the African continent and conducted camps for boys and girls between the ages of 7 and 12.
In addition to hosting camps, Adeola Ajayi Foundations also planned on building basketball courts and conducted outreach programs in communities of need throughout Africa.
Adeola Ajayi Fo
Our mission is to use basketball as a means to educate and enrich the lives of African kids. We aim to provide quality facilities, gear and coaches with the goal of growing the game of basketball within Africa. We create awareness and support for underprivileged children and young adults through our camps, which place emphasis on hard work, accountability, honest living and positivity. We strive to create opportunity not only as a player, but also a well rounded citizens.
